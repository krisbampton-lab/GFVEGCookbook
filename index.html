Smoky Three-Bean Chipotle Chili

Thick, smoky, high-protein chili built for big appetite, toppings, and freezing in bricks.

Serves 6–8 Prep: 15 min Cook: 30–40 min
GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan High-Protein Freezer-FriendlyMeal prep OK

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
Spices & flavour
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½–1 tsp ground coriander (optional)
  • 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo, minced
  • 1 tsp adobo sauce from the can
  • 1 tsp sugar or maple syrup
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
Liquids & beans
  • 2 cans (14 oz / 398 ml) crushed or diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can pinto or navy beans, drained & rinsed
  • 2–3 cups vegetable broth
  • Juice of 1 lime
To serve (optional)
  • Shredded cheese, sour cream or yogurt
  • Sliced green onions, chopped cilantro
  • Avocado, crushed GF tortilla chips

How to use it
  • Storage: Fridge 3–4 days; freezer up to 2–3 months in sealed containers.
  • Reheat: Stovetop over low heat with a splash of water/stock; or microwave in short bursts, stirring.
  • Serve with: GF bread / flatbread, simple salad, roasted veg, or over rice/quinoa for extra bulk. Try Quick GF Yogurt Skillet Flatbreads or GF Skillet Cornbread from this book.
  • Variations: Adjust spice and thickness with extra stock or coconut milk; easy to add beans or fake meat at the end.
  • Meal prep: Keeps well for 3–4 days in the fridge in sealed containers. Let it cool fully before lid goes on, and reheat gently with a splash of water or broth if it looks dry.
  • Regular gluten / non-vegetarian option (for meal prep): For mixed eaters, keep the base as written and cook meat once in a separate pan. Plan about 350–400 g (12–14 oz) cooked chicken, sausage, or lean ground meat per full recipe batch. Use regular wheat pasta / bread / tortillas where the recipe calls for GF versions, and spoon the meat over individual portions so you don’t need a second pot of sauce.

Method

  1. Heat oil in a large heavy pot over medium. Add onion, carrot, celery, and bell pepper. Cook 8–10 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
  2. Stir in garlic and cook 1 minute. Add tomato paste and all spices. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add chipotle, adobo, and tomatoes, scraping up any browned bits.
  4. Add beans, 2 cups broth, sugar or maple, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then simmer on low 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more broth if too thick.
  6. Adjust seasoning; stir in lime juice at the end.
  7. Serve with toppings. Even better the next day; freezes well.

Vegan: Use plant-based toppings or skip dairy.

Storage: 4–5 days in the fridge, 3 months in the freezer.

Creamy Roasted Cauliflower & White Bean Soup

Roasted cauliflower blended with white beans and broth into a silky, satisfying soup that actually fills you up.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 10–15 min Cook: 25–30 min
GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan CreamyMeal prep OK

Ingredients

Soup base
  • 1 large head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 3 tbsp olive oil, divided
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1 can (14 oz) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • ½–1 cup milk or cream (or unsweetened plant milk)
  • 1–2 tbsp nutritional yeast or grated Parmesan (optional)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
To finish
  • Extra olive oil, for drizzling
  • Chopped parsley or chives
  • Toasted pumpkin seeds (optional)

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss cauliflower with 2 tbsp oil, salt, and pepper. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once.
  2. Sauté onion in remaining 1 tbsp oil 7–8 minutes. Add garlic, thyme, smoked paprika; cook 1 minute.
  3. Add roasted cauliflower, beans, and broth. Simmer 10–15 minutes.
  4. Blend until smooth (stick blender or regular blender in batches).
  5. Stir in milk/cream and nutritional yeast/Parmesan. Warm gently 5 minutes.
  6. Add lemon juice; adjust salt and pepper.
  7. Serve with olive oil, herbs, and seeds.

Vegan: Use plant milk and nutritional yeast.

Storage: 3–4 days in the fridge; loosen with water when reheating.

  7. Serve with olive oil/yogurt and herbs.

Texture: Lightly mash or pulse-blend if you want it creamier.

Storage: 4–5 days in the fridge; 3 months in the freezer.

Creamy Potato, Leek & Kale Soup

Classic potato–leek soup with extra greens and protein from white beans. Comfort in a bowl.

GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan CreamyMeal prep OK

Ingredients

  • 5 cups vegetable broth

  6. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice if desired.

Vegan: Use olive oil and plant milk.

  • 2 tbsp tomato paste

  • 1 tsp dried rosemary

  • Storage: Fridge 3–4 days; many mains freeze well in meal-sized containers.
