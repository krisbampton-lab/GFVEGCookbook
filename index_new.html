Smoky Three-Bean Chipotle Chili

Thick, smoky, high-protein chili built for big appetite, lots of toppings, and freezing in bricks.

Serves 6–8 Prep: 15 min Cook: 30–40 min
GFVegetarianEasily VeganHigh-ProteinFreezer-Friendly

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
Spices & flavour
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp chili powder (GF)
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½–1 tsp ground coriander (optional)
  • 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo, minced
  • 1–2 tsp adobo sauce from the can
  • 1 tsp sugar or maple syrup
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
Liquids & beans
  • 2 cans (398 ml) crushed or diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained & rinsed
  • 1 can pinto or navy beans, drained & rinsed
  • 2–3 cups vegetable broth
  • Juice of 1 lime
To serve (optional)
  • Shredded cheese or vegan cheese
  • Greek yogurt / sour cream or DF yogurt
  • Sliced green onions, cilantro, avocado, crushed GF tortilla chips
Nutrition & usage

Per large bowl (≈1.5–2 cups, without toppings)

Protein≈18–22 g
Carbs≈40–50 g
Fats≈10–14 g
Fibre≈10–13 g

Add tortilla chips / rice and cheese and the numbers climb fast — easy to scale up/down per person.

Meal prep & storage
  • Keeps 4 days in the fridge; 2–3 months in the freezer.
  • Cool completely before freezing; portion into bricks or individual containers.
Reheating
  • Stovetop low heat with a splash of water or broth, 5–10 min.
  • Microwave in 60–90 second bursts, stirring between.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • For non-veg eater: brown 250–350 g ground beef/turkey in a separate pan with 1–2 tsp chili powder.
  • Stir their meat into their portion only; base stays fully veg and GF.
Serve with / ideas
  • Over rice or quinoa for heavier meals.
  • On top of baked potatoes or wedges (ties into Combined Meals section nicely).

Method

  1. Heat oil in a large heavy pot over medium. Add onion, carrot, celery, and bell pepper. Cook 8–10 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
  2. Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, and coriander. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add chipotle, adobo sauce, and tomatoes. Scrape up any browned bits from the bottom.
  4. Add beans, 2 cups broth, sugar/maple, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil, then simmer on low 25–35 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add more broth if too thick.
  6. Taste and adjust salt, heat, and sweetness. Stir in lime juice right at the end.
  7. Serve hot with toppings. Even better the next day.

Creamy Roasted Cauliflower & White Bean Soup

Silky, slightly nutty, very filling. White beans bring protein and body; roasting the cauliflower does the flavour work.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 35–40 min
GFVegetarianEasily VeganHigh-Fibre

Ingredients

Roast tray
  • 1 large head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt & pepper
Soup base
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • 1 onion, sliced or diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 can (398 ml) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • ½–1 cup milk, cream, or unsweetened plant milk
  • 1–2 tsp lemon juice
  • Optional: 2 tbsp grated Parmesan or nutritional yeast
To finish
  • Extra olive oil, cracked pepper
  • Chopped chives or parsley
Nutrition & usage

Per medium bowl (≈1.5 cups, with plant milk)

Protein≈10–12 g
Carbs≈20–25 g
Fats≈10–14 g
Fibre≈6–8 g

Use cream instead of plant milk if you want it richer and slightly higher calorie.

Meal prep & storage
  • Keeps 3–4 days chilled; 1–2 months frozen if blended very smooth.
  • Soup may thicken in the fridge; thin with broth or water when reheating.
Reheating
  • Stovetop on low, stirring; don’t boil hard, especially if using dairy.
  • Microwave in short bursts, stirring to avoid hot spots.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • Serve theirs with buttered regular bread / grilled cheese; yours with GF bread.
  • You can blend in extra Parmesan for their bowl at the table.
Serve with / ideas
  • Crusty GF toast, grilled cheese, roasted chickpeas on top.
  • Pairs well with bright salads or anything acidic to cut the richness.

Method

  1. Heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss cauliflower with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a tray. Roast 25–30 minutes until browned and sweet at the edges.
  2. Meanwhile, in a pot, heat 1 tbsp oil/butter over medium. Sauté onion 6–8 minutes until soft and golden. Add garlic and cook 30–60 seconds.
  3. Add roasted cauliflower, white beans, and broth. Simmer 10 minutes to let flavours mingle.
  4. Blend with an immersion blender until smooth (or carefully in batches in a blender).
  5. Stir in milk/cream/plant milk and optional Parmesan/yeast. Adjust salt, pepper, and lemon to taste.
  6. Serve hot, drizzled with olive oil and topped with herbs.

Hearty Red Lentil & Vegetable Soup

Big, chunky, and comforting. Red lentils break down and thicken the broth; veggies keep it interesting.

Serves 6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 25–30 min
GFVegetarianHigh-ProteinHigh-FibreMeal prep OK

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
Lentils & liquid
  • 1½ cups red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (398 ml) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
Seasoning
  • 1½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme or oregano
  • ½ tsp turmeric (optional)
  • Salt & pepper
  • Juice of ½–1 lemon
Optional add-ins
  • 2 cups chopped greens (kale, spinach)
  • 1 small potato or sweet potato, diced
Nutrition & usage

Per large bowl (≈2 cups)

Protein≈18–20 g
Carbs≈40–45 g
Fats≈6–8 g
Fibre≈10–12 g
Meal prep & storage
  • Holds 4–5 days in the fridge; 2 months frozen.
  • Soup will thicken a lot as it sits — just add water when reheating.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • Serve theirs with regular naan/bread and yours with GF options.
  • They can add shredded rotisserie chicken into their bowl if they want extra protein.
Use it as:
  • A straight soup with bread or rice.
  • A stew over mashed potatoes or baked potatoes.

Method

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery 6–8 minutes until softened.
  2. Add garlic; cook 30 seconds.
  3. Stir in red lentils, tomatoes, broth/water, and spices. Add potatoes now if using.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook 18–25 minutes until lentils are soft and starting to break down.
  5. If using greens, stir them in for the last 5 minutes.
  6. Season generously with salt, pepper, and lemon juice to brighten.

Creamy Potato, Leek & Kale Soup

Classic potato-leek comfort but heavier on fibre and colour thanks to kale. Can be chunky or mostly blended.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 25–30 min
GFVegetarianComfort

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 2 leeks, white and light green parts, sliced and rinsed well
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled if you want, diced
  • 5 cups vegetable broth
Greens & cream
  • 2 cups chopped kale, stems removed (or spinach)
  • ½–1 cup milk, cream, or plant milk
  • Salt & pepper
  • Optional: ½ cup shredded cheese for extra richness
Nutrition & usage

Per medium bowl (≈1.5 cups, with plant milk)

Protein≈6–8 g
Carbs≈30–35 g
Fats≈8–12 g
Fibre≈4–6 g
Meal prep & storage
  • Keeps 3 days chilled; dairy-heavy versions can split slightly but stir back together.
  • Freezing is fine but the texture may be a bit grainy — still good for work lunches.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • Serve theirs with buttered regular toast or grilled ham & cheese.
  • Stir extra cheese into their bowl at the table.

Method

  1. In a pot over medium, melt butter/heat oil. Add sliced leeks and cook 8–10 minutes until soft but not browned.
  2. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds.
  3. Add potatoes and broth. Bring to a boil, then simmer 15–20 minutes until potatoes are very tender.
  4. Lightly blend with an immersion blender (just a few pulses) if you want a creamier base.
  5. Add kale and simmer 5 minutes more until tender.
  6. Stir in milk/cream/plant milk and cheese if using. Season generously with salt and pepper.

Tuscan White Bean & Kale Soup

Brothy, olive oil–forward, lots of garlic and greens. Rustic but still feels light.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 25 min
GFVegetarianHigh-Fibre

Ingredients

Base
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
Beans & broth
  • 2 cans (398 ml each) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1–2 tsp dried Italian herbs (oregano, basil, thyme)
  • 1 bay leaf
Greens & finish
  • 3–4 cups chopped kale
  • 2–3 tbsp lemon juice
  • Extra olive oil, Parmesan or nutritional yeast (optional)
  • Salt & pepper
Nutrition & usage

Per large bowl

Protein≈14–16 g
Carbs≈30–35 g
Fats≈7–10 g
Fibre≈9–11 g
Meal prep & storage
  • 4 days in the fridge; beans keep texture well.
  • Freezer-friendly up to 2 months.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • Serve theirs with regular garlic bread; yours with GF.
  • If you ever make Italian sausage, they can slice that into their bowl only.

Method

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery 8 minutes until softened.
  2. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds.
  3. Add beans, broth, Italian herbs, bay leaf, and a good pinch of salt.
  4. Simmer 15 minutes. Add kale and cook 5–10 minutes more, until tender but still green.
  5. Finish with lemon juice, extra olive oil, black pepper, and Parmesan/yeast if using.

Spiced Butternut Squash & Apple Soup

Slightly sweet, gently spiced, and smooth. Good gateway soup for people who ignore vegetables.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 20 min Cook: 30–35 min
GFVegetarianFreezer-Friendly

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 butternut squash, peeled and cubed (≈1.2–1.5 kg whole)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 apples (e.g., Gala or Honeycrisp), peeled and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
Liquid & spices
  • 4–5 cups vegetable broth
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of cayenne or chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt & pepper
  • ½–1 cup coconut milk or cream/milk
Finish
  • Pepitas, toasted nuts, swirl of yogurt or coconut cream
Nutrition & usage

Per medium bowl (with coconut milk)

Protein≈4–6 g
Carbs≈30–35 g
Fats≈10–14 g
Fibre≈4–6 g
Meal prep & storage
  • Keeps 4 days chilled; 2–3 months frozen.
  • Great as a starter or a lighter dinner with grilled cheese / toast.
Mixed-diet tweak
  • Serve theirs with regular grilled cheese; yours with GF version.

Method

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium. Sauté onion 5–7 minutes until soft.
  2. Add squash and apples; cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add garlic, cinnamon, nutmeg, cayenne if using, salt, and pepper; cook 1 minute.
  4. Add 4 cups broth and bring to a simmer. Cook 20–25 minutes until squash is very soft.
  5. Blend until smooth. Add coconut milk or cream; thin with extra broth if needed.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve with toppings.

Loaded Vegetable "Barley" Soup (with GF Grain)

Classic veg + “barley” soup but actually gluten free by using millet, buckwheat, or short-grain brown rice.

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
Grain & broth
  • ¾ cup millet, buckwheat, or short-grain brown rice (rinsed)
  • 7–8 cups vegetable broth
Veg & seasoning
  • 1 cup green beans or peas
  • 1 cup corn
  • 1 can (398 ml) diced tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt & pepper

Per bowl

Protein≈9–11 g
Carbs≈35–45 g
Fats≈5–7 g
Fibre≈6–8 g

Thai-Inspired Peanut & Sweet Potato Stew

Thick, peanutty, mildly spicy stew with sweet potato chunks and greens. Almost a curry, almost a stew.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 20 min Cook: 25–30 min
GFVegetarianHigh-Energy

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste (GF)
Body
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • ¼–⅓ cup natural peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp soy/tamari (GF)
  • 1–2 tbsp lime juice
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or kale
Nutrition & usage

Per hearty bowl (without rice)

Protein≈12–15 g
Carbs≈35–40 g
Fats≈22–26 g
Fibre≈6–8 g
Meal prep
  • 3–4 days chilled; 1–2 months frozen.
  • Sweet potatoes reheat well without going mushy if not overcooked the first time.

Method

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium. Sauté onion 5–7 minutes until soft.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and curry paste; cook 1–2 minutes.
  3. Add sweet potatoes, coconut milk, and broth. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Whisk in peanut butter and tamari/soy until smooth.
  5. Simmer 20–25 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Stir in greens for last 3–5 minutes. Finish with lime juice and adjust seasoning.

Creamy White Bean & Roasted Garlic Soup

Smooth, garlicky, and rich-tasting without tons of dairy. Beans carry the weight.

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min (+roasting) Cook: 30–35 min
GFVegetarianHigh-Fibre

Ingredients

Roasted garlic
  • 1 whole head garlic
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Salt
Soup base
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cans (398 ml each) white beans, drained & rinsed
  • 4–5 cups vegetable broth
  • ½–1 cup milk, cream, or plant milk
  • Salt & pepper
  • Lemon juice to finish
Nutrition & usage

Per bowl

Protein≈12–14 g
Carbs≈25–30 g
Fats≈8–12 g
Fibre≈7–9 g
Meal prep
  • 3–4 days chilled; freezes well.
  • Good base for “soup + toast” lunches.

Method

  1. Heat oven to 400°F (200°C). Slice top off garlic head, drizzle with oil, sprinkle salt, wrap in foil, and roast 30–35 minutes until soft.
  2. In a pot, heat oil and cook onion 8–10 minutes until soft and lightly golden.
  3. Squeeze roasted garlic cloves out of their skins into the pot; discard skins.
  4. Add beans and 4 cups broth. Simmer 10–15 minutes.
  5. Blend until smooth, then add milk/cream/plant milk. Thin with extra broth if needed.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and a little lemon juice.

Mushroom & Lentil Shepherd’s Pie

Deeply savoury lentil and mushroom filling under a thick layer of golden mashed potatoes.

Serves 6–8 Prep: 25–30 min Bake: 25–30 min
GF Vegetarian Easily Vegan Freezer-Friendly

Ingredients

Potato topping
  • 1.5 kg (about 3 lb) potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 3–4 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • ½–¾ cup milk or plant milk
  • Salt & pepper
Filling
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 400 g (about 14 oz) mushrooms, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½ cups dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tbsp GF tamari or soy sauce
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (plus a bit more if needed)
  • 1–2 tsp Worcestershire-style sauce (GF/veg or skip)
  • Salt & black pepper

  • Storage: Fridge 3–4 days; many mains freeze well in meal-sized containers.
  • Reheat: Oven or stovetop over medium-low heat; add a splash of water or sauce if it looks dry.
  • Serve with: Side salad, steamed greens, or roasted veg; add bread/flatbread or rice for heavier appetites.
  • Variations: Swap beans/lentils ↔ fake meat; for meat-eaters, cook meat separately and fold into their portions at the end.

Method

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water 15–20 minutes until tender. Drain; mash with butter/oil and milk. Season.
  2. Sauté onion, carrots, celery in oil 8–10 minutes.
  3. Add mushrooms and salt; cook 8–10 minutes until most liquid evaporates.
  4. Add garlic, lentils, tomato paste, herbs, smoked paprika, and tamari; cook 1–2 minutes.
  5. Add broth and Worcestershire; simmer covered 25–30 minutes, adding more liquid if needed, until lentils are tender and thick.
  6. Season filling; transfer to baking dish.
  7. Top with potatoes; rough up surface with a fork.
  8. Bake at 400°F (200°C) 25–30 minutes until golden and bubbling. Rest 10 minutes.

Vegan: Use olive oil and plant milk in mash; vegan Worcestershire or omit.

Makes ~1½ cups Prep: 10 min No cook

Ingredients

  • ½ cup smooth peanut butter
  • 2–3 tbsp tamari or gluten-free soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1–2 tbsp maple syrup or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 1 small clove garlic, minced
  • ½–1 tsp chili flakes or sriracha (optional)
  • ¼–½ cup warm water, to thin

How to use it
  • Storage: Fridge 4–5 days in a jar; some sauces freeze well in small portions or ice cube trays.
  • Reheat: Gently on the stovetop or in short microwave bursts; whisk in a spoon of water if too thick.
  • Serve with: Over bowls, pasta, roasted veg, fake meatballs/sausages, or as a bread-dipping sauce.
  • Variations: Adjust thickness with water/stock; vary herbs and heat level; for mixed diners, use the same sauces on veg and meat.

Method

  1. Whisk peanut butter, tamari, lime juice, maple syrup, ginger, garlic, and chili (if using) in a bowl.
  2. Gradually whisk in warm water until smooth and pourable.
  3. Taste and adjust salt, sweetness, and heat to your liking.

Flourless Chocolate Almond Cake

Rich, fudgy, naturally gluten-free chocolate cake made with ground almonds.

Serves 8 Prep: 20 min Bake: 25–30 min

Ingredients

  • 200 g dark chocolate (ensure GF), chopped
  • 150 g butter or coconut oil
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 4 large eggs, room temp
  • 1½ cups fine almond flour
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
Nutrition & usage▾

Quick Snapshot

Serving size≈1 slice / portion / bowl
Primary focusTreat: carbs + fats
Protein≈4–8 g
Carbs≈35–45 g
Fats≈12–20 g
Fibre≈3–6 g
Vitamins / mineralsFruit-based: vit C & potassium; nut/oat-based: magnesium & vitamin E
How to use it
  • Storage: Most keep 3–4 days chilled; some bars and oat bakes freeze well in squares.
  • Reheat: Serve chilled, room temp, or gently warmed; add ice cream, yogurt, or whipped cream if you like.
  • Serve with: Coffee, tea, or fresh fruit on the side; pair warm desserts with something cold for contrast.
  • Variations: Dial sweetness, switch fruits, add nuts or seeds; keep the base GF vegetarian, and avoid hidden gelatine.

Method

  1. Heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line and grease an 8–9 in (20–23 cm) springform pan.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter together gently; cool slightly.
  3. Whisk sugar and eggs until slightly thickened.
  4. Stir in melted chocolate mixture, then almond flour, cocoa, vanilla, and salt.
  5. Pour into pan; bake 25–30 minutes until just set in the centre. Cool before slicing.
